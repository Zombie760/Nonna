## About the Series

Meet Regina Russo — 52 years old, grandmother of two, and the person Harbor View calls when something needs to be found. To her grandkids, she's "Nonna G," the warm, funny, sauce-making grandmother who just wants Sunday dinners and peace. To the town's old crowd, she's still "G" — the same survivor who learned that some boxes shouldn't be opened, and some shouldn't stay closed.

Every book begins as a simple case in the quiet coastal town of Harbor View, California. But each case quietly leads Regina back into the same invisible patterns of power that never pay their debts. She never shouts "conspiracy." She just keeps finding the same hand on the ledger — while trying desperately to stay in "Nonna" mode for her grandkids.

**Tone:** Pure Sue Grafton — dry humor, sharp observation, deep affection for everyday people, quiet moral clarity, and the feeling that you're sitting in a real kitchen with a real person who has lived a real life.

## Reading Order

The Regina Russo series is designed to be read in order, with each book building on the last:

1. **A is for Archive** — The case that pulls Regina out of retirement
2. **B is for Bloodline** — Family secrets and inherited debts
3. **C is for Custody** — A child caught between power and protection
